Rules
Everyone is subject to the same three strikes system. Breaking one rule gives you one strike. Failing to comply with rules such as no advertising or no hacking will result in receiving three strikes. It should also be noted that if a player joins the server for the first time to immediately start causing trouble, i.e. they random death match everyone, we may just perma ban them.
Strike 1: Warning/ Mute.
Strike 2: Jail/ Tempban.
Strike 3: Permanent Ban.

General Rules

Basic Rules
1. No griefing or harassment.
2. Using modded clients or "hacks" will result in a ban. Same goes for "texture packs" that let you see through blocks.
3. Alternate accounts are not allowed.
4. No random death matches, except at the PVP arena.
5. Using Limited Creative to fly to other nations or continents is not allowed. Doing so may result in you being jailed or banned.
6. Mob grinders, automatic farms, and other lag machines are not allowed on this server.
7. Use of banned items and/or enchantments is prohibited, and may result in said items being confiscated, or you being banned.
8. Attempting to farm hundreds or even dozens of animals in a small area is not allowed as this causes lag and reduces the number of wild animals that spawn in the world for other players to hunt. You can keep a few milk cows or sheep but mass producing mobs will result in most of the mobs being killed by staff, and repeat offenders may be banned. If you have over a dozen animals in one pen or in multiple pens in a small area they will be at least partly removed or in extreme cases of abuse completely.
9. The use of Explosive Pickaxes for any kind of griefing, or destroying enemy walls/structures/ships/etc. will result in a Permanent Ban.
10. Using glitched guns for ANY reason will result in your inventory being completely cleared and a temporary ban.
11. You may not file any kind of application in the name of another player. It does not matter if they are unable to file the application themselves for whatever reason.
12. You may not use schematics mods.


Chat Rules

Chat Rules
1. Racism is not permitted (except in RP).
2. Homophobia, heterophobia and transphobia are not permitted (except in RP).
3. You may discuss real world politics on the server, but keep it friendly.
4. Spam is of course a no.
5. So is advertising your Minecraft server or crappy product.
6. Do not argue with staff on the server. If you disagree with a decision you may express that on the forums.
7. Excessive swearing is not permitted.
8. Just don't be a jerk.


Nation Rules

Nation Rules:
1. Builds must be realistic, fit the time period, and look nice.
2. Staff members WILL remove leaders who fail to build up their nation or become inactive.
3. If a nation leader quits the server they automatically surrender any wars they are involved in.
4. Nation leaders may not just hand leadership over to a friend. Their successor must apply for the nation just as they normally would.
5. Setting your home in another nation without the leader's consent is a bannable offense.
5 a. This goes for the Merchant nation too.
6. The selling of land to foreign governments is forbidden.


Assassination Rules

Assassination Rules:
1. You must apply to assassinate someone, else it will be called a random death match. Applications are private and regular players cannot see them.
2. You cannot assassinate a leader if they are in their peaceweek.
3. If a you kill a nation leader, that person does not have to abandon their faction OOC, but must make a new character.
4. At least one other person in the targets nation must be online at the time of being struck.
5. Nation leaders cannot perform assassinations. They must have someone else do it.


Banditry Rules

Banditry/Piracy Rules
1. An outlaw group is limited to 4 players maximum.
2. You must wait two days before raiding the same place.
3. The only building you can damage are city walls.
4. Normal naval rules apply.
5. You may not participate in full battles.
6. The defenders must have at least 3 people online.
7. You must make a thread about your group before you gain outlaw status. Failure to do so will result in your raids being noted as random death matches.
8. If all your members die during a raid, you may never raid that place again.
9. You cannot ally an outlaw group.


Alliance Rules

Alliance Rules 1. A nation can not have any more than 2 allies.
2. Only the primary attacking and defending nations may bring their allies into a war. Those allies may not bring additional allies.
3. In order to form an alliance with another nation you must both construct embassies for each other in your own nations.
4. Nation leaders who do not honor alliances they made can be denounced and their alliances severed.
5. A nation leader who has been denounced for any reason cannot form new alliances until the denouncement expires. (1 week)
6. If a nation's leader has been denounced you may sever any alliances you have with them with no penalty.


Vassal Rules

Vassal Rules
1. The leader of a vassal nation must answer directly to the leader of the owning nation.
2. A vassal may not declare war on its owning nation. There is one exception to this.
2a. A vassal can declare war on its owning nation IF they have been in some way transgressed upon.
3. Declaring war on a vassal means also declaring war on its owning nation, and vice-versa.
4. Applications for vassal nations should be accepted by the owning nation, as well as a neutral staff member.


Roleplay Rules

Roleplay Rules
1. No metagaming. (Using OOC knowledge while IC.)
2. You must have a character to RP. The only exception is that non-leaders may play as an unnamed soldier or guard during battles and raids.
3. All involved parties must consent for erotic, torture, or slave rp. Attempting to force any of these on another player without consent will result in a ban.
4. Any role play events may be voided by staff or if all involved parties consent to it.
5. All PvP outside of a war must have a reason to it within Role Play.
6. If you are caught trespassing in a nation you are not at war with, and they ask you to leave, you must do so. If you do not they may attack you.
7. Blocking the passage of a nation through the wilderness or their own territory is also a valid reason to denounce someone.
8. So is mobilizing an army or navy at someone's border, or building defensive structures near their border such as walls, trenches, fortresses, cannons, etc.
9. Any other action in role play which could be considered hostile toward other nations may be reason for their leaders to denounce you.
10. You may only denounce another nation's leader for actions they have taken in role play. You may not denounce someone for historical or personal reasons. Nor may you blame actions they have not taken on them.
11. Nation leaders who make frivolous denouncements in an attempt to warmonger or harass other players will be punished by having their denouncement voided and being denounced themselves, along with severing all alliances they currently hold. Example: "I'm denouncing you because you suck at RP and I don't like you."
12. Nation leaders must conduct themselves according to their government type.
13. Please ask staff before role playing anything outside the current time period. We will allow some minor things but for example, you can't invent electricity or airplanes in the 1700's.


Movecraft Rules

Movecraft Rules
1. All ships, hot air balloons, etc. must look at least somewhat realistic.
2. You can not use dispensers to shoot TNT, Flame Charges, etc.
3. You may not place cannons on hot air balloons as they would be too heavy realistically.
4. You may throw grenades and shoot guns from hot air balloons during raids or battles.
5. You are responsible for your own vessel so be careful not to crash it.
6. Do not abuse Movecraft too much as it can cause lag, try to use it sparingly for RP purposes.


Native Rules

Native Rules
1. Natives may not use Shields or wear Armor.
2. Natives may only use guns they have purchased. They may not craft them.
3. Only Natives may use Blowguns and Tomahawks.
4. At /warp PVP, non-Natives may use Native builds (no armor, tomahawks, etc.) and Natives may use non-Native builds. (armor, guns, shield, etc.) You may not mix the two though by using a tomahawk/blowgun and also wearing armor or using a shield.
5. The minimum number of required players does not apply to Natives for raids/battles/etc. Natives may also raid their enemy whenever they like during war regardless of how many players they have on.
6. Natives may not use cannons/mortars, grenades, or any other kind of explosive.
7. Natives must create historically accurate builds for their tribe. They may not construct steam boats, steam wagons, hot air balloons, etc. They may only create small ships, tents, wooden huts, etc.


Donator Rules

Donator Rules:
1. You cannot have profanity in your nick/RP name.
2. You cannot use lc creative to fly over other people's walls/ cities/ settlements.
3. Do not go into gamemode creative during RP or PvP/ PvE.
4. Creative should be used for building and nothing else.
5. You cannot spawn in items for other players.


War Rules
War Rules Terms:
Active Player- player who comes online at least every other day for a period of an hour or more and have been a member of one nation for at least 7 days
Raid- a small group from one nation going to “surprise attack” another nation at a location without the use of heavy armor.

War Rules:
1. Section 1: Main Nations
a. A nation may only declare 1 war on a nation at a time (to attack). This does not limit how many nations may declare war on a single nation, however, so playing the big kid on the block may not be the best strategy (Yes, you can be attacked by 8 nations at the same time as long as they aren’t breaking the ally cap).
b. Before declaring war, the attacking nation must denounce the opposing nation. The denunciation must be realistic and valid.
c. You cannot declare war on a nation until 7 days after they have their first leader.
d. At the completion of a war, if a leader was captured or killed, that nation cannot enter into a new war for 7 days after a new leader is chosen.
e. In the event of a war, both the attacking nation and defending nation must have 3 active players each.
f. If a leader quits the server, the nation will be led by a staff member or the former leader’s second in command, unless a new leader has already been appointed.
i. If the nation still has more than 3 active members, the war will continue
ii. If the nation has less than 3 active members, the opposing nation wins the war
iii. If the second in command comes into power, they may only lead the nation until the end of the war. After that they must apply for said nation.
g. A nation may only have 2 allies that may participate in a war.
h. Everything must stay relevant to the current time period, so no cobweb barriers or walls made of lava.

2. Section 2: Allies
a. Should an ally of the attacking nation choose to participate in a war as an ally, they do not need to denounce nor have another valid reason to declare war on the defending nation.
b. The ally must have at least 2 active players in order to participate in the war.
3. Section 3: Vassals
a. Vassals are not considered allies
b. Can be called to war as part of the nation that made it a vassal, however, should the vassal be the attacking nation, the nation that made it a vassal will be counted as one of its allies
4. Section 4: Mercenaries
a. May only participate in 1 battle for every 3 battles that happen, (i.e. Battle 1: With mercenary, Battles 2 & 3: Without Mercenary, Battle 4: With Mercenary, Battles 5 & 6: Without Mercenary, etc.)
b. Must be a rp character or a peasant (Leaders, Government Figures, and War related rp characters cannot be mercenaries)
c. May not participate in raids
5. Section 5: Griefing
a. The complete or partial destroying of non-military related builds or farms within your own territory during a war is considered griefing. 6. Section 6: Winning a war
a. War may be won by one of the following ways:
i. One of the Nations surrender and a Peace Treaty is signed
ii. Either nation earns a total of 5 War Points (a nation may have a negative number of War Points)
iii. The defending nation having 3 War Points while the attacking nation has a zero War Points.
iv. A nation loses all of its cities (total conquest)

Battle Rules:
1. Section 1: Non-city Land Battles
a. A battle must be fought on the border between the two nations. Should the nations not share a land border, the nation must be invaded by sea. Should the nation not have either one of these, other nations must consent to the movement of troops through their territory.
b. A battle time must be agreed upon on the war application thread.
i. Stalling to agree on a battle time will result in removal from leadership from whoever is causing the delay.
c. The attacking nation may bring 50% more players than the defending nation to a battle (if the 50% causes a non-whole number, the number will be rounded down).
d. The defenders must have at least 2 active players online
e. Players participating in the battle must battle as their rp character if their character is related in any way to the army. Regular civilians may fight as unnamed soldiers.
f. If an rp character is “killed” during battle, the opposing side may declare the rp character dead or captured. The player may participate in future battles as unnamed soldiers.
g. Once a rp player is “killed or captured,” he/she may not enter into the battle again. Any kills made after being killed are considered void and will cause that player’s nation to lose half of a War Point.
h. Any aspect of limited creative mode may not be used by a player during a battle. Violation of this rule will result in that nation losing that battle, and will lose an additional 1 War Point (so 2 total War Points) as well as that player will have limited creative permanently removed. They may also be banned, depending on the circumstances.
i. The nation that wins the battle gains 1 War Point and will be able to claim a 10x10 chunk of the enemy territory around the battle area.
j. The nation that loses the battle loses 1 War Point

Section 2: Naval Battles
a. See “Battle Rules, Section 1: Non-City Land Battles, b-e, g-i
b. Players participating in the battle must battle as their rp character if their character is related in any way to the Navy. Regular civilians may fight as unnamed soldiers
c. Once a ship is taking water, it is considered sunk.
d. All battle tactics must be realistic (ie. No using water buckets to board enemy vessels)
e. A blockade consists of 2 or more ships and a battle must be fought in order to get past the blockade
f. Each attacking ship must have at least 1 player.
g. A ship is considered open when it is not occupied by at least 1 player
h. The battle is over when one nation no longer has a non-sunk ship or when all of its players are dead.
i. The nation that wins gains .5 War Points and
i. If attacking can pass the blockade
ii. If defending, maintains the blockade
j. The nation that loses, loses .5 War Points
3. Section 3: Coastal Battles
a. See “Battle Rules, Section 1: Non-City Land Battles, f
b. See “Battle Rules, Section 2: Naval Battles, a-d, f, and g
c. A battle is over once all players on one side are “killed or captured” or the attacking nation has all its ships sunk
d. If the attacking nation wins, they gain .5 War Points and a 5x5 chunk of land on the coast located at the area of the attack. The location of the attack must be a realistic landing point (i.e. a nation cannot perform a landing on the side of a cliff). The defending nation loses .5 War Points
e. If the defending nation wins, they gain 1 War Point and the attacking nation loses 1 War Point.

4. Section 4: City Battles
a. See “Battle Rules, Section 1: Non-City Land Battles, a-i
b. The border between the two nations must be next to a city for an attacking nation be able to attack the city.
c. To declare a city captured, all defenders must be killed or captured as well as the attacking nation’s banner/flag must be placed in a visible location somewhere in the city.
d. The nation that wins a City Battle gains 2 War Points and is awarded the conquered city and everything inside of it, being able to claim all of it for their faction.
e. The nation that loses a City Battle loses 2 War Points and must immediately leave the city as is. All locked chests will be unlocked by staff for use by the new owners.
Raid Rules:

Section 1: Raiding Party
a. See “Battle Rules, Section 1: Non-City Land Battles, c, e, and h.
b. A raiding party must consist of at least 3 people but no more than 5
c. Raids happen only in/around cities, ports, or forts.
d. When a player dies in a raid, he is killed. RP characters cannot be captured during a raid.
e. If all defenders are killed, the raiding nation gains 1 War Point and must immediately make an rp post in the forums.
2. Section 2: Defenders
a. See “Battle Rules, Section 1: Non-City Land Battles, h, and i b. The defenders may use previously placed cannons against the raiding party (i.e. a defender cannot make a new cannon during a raid).
c. If all members of the raiding party are killed, the raiding nation loses .5 War Points and must immediately make an rp post in the forums.

End of War Negotiations
1. Defending nation has 3 points while attacking nation has zero points
a. To negotiate a peace treaty, a neutral location will provide all the nations with a meeting place in order to discuss the terms. If a meeting place cannot be agreed on by the players, then staff will select one for them.
b. All official negotiations will be done within the local chat at the predetermined location
c. All participating nations may only send 1 representative to discuss the terms.
d. If no final treaty can be made at the conclusion of the first meeting, a second one will be held at a later date.
e. There shall be no more than 3 meetings held
f. Should no decision be made after 3 meetings, 1 representative each from 3 neutral nations (nations shall be agreed upon by the staff team) will decide the final terms of the treaty.
